About Me

My name is Tonian (pronunciation: TOH-nEE-ANN), a Ph.D. candidate at the University of South Florida, where I study sinkhole and other anthropogenic-related deformations using Persistent Scatterer Interferometric Synthetic Aperature (PSInSAR). My current project focuses on analyzing satellite images collected in West-Central Florida to improve the detection of sinkhole deformation signals. I enjoy data processing and application development, especially using my GIS and remote sensing-related skills.

How I became a Geophysicist

My love for field began after taking a Geophysics course. In 2014 I began working as a part-time Undergraduate Research Assistant in Near-surface Geophysics. The project I worked on focused on estimating the petrophysical properties controlling fluid flow of sandstone samples using geophysical applications.
